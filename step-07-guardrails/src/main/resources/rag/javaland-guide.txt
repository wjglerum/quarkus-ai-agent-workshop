JavaLand 2026 Conference Guide — Phantasialand, Brühl, Germany

§A Venue & Logistics
A.1 JavaLand 2026 takes place at Phantasialand theme park in Brühl, Germany, about 20 minutes by train from Cologne.
A.2 Conference dates are March 9-12, 2026. Monday March 9 is the Sternfahrt (pre-event gathering) and the Community UnKonferenz.
A.3 Tuesday March 10 and Wednesday March 11 are the main conference days with talks, keynotes, and community sessions.
A.4 Thursday March 12 features full-day hands-on workshops. These require separate registration.
A.5 Brühl is easily accessible from Cologne Hauptbahnhof via regional train (line RB48 or RE5). The Phantasialand entrance is a short walk from Brühl station.

§B Session Rooms
B.1 Dome — The main stage used for keynotes and high-capacity talks.
B.2 Bellevue — Architecture & Methodology sessions; a mid-sized room for design and process talks.
B.3 Sanssouci — Java Language & Community track; great for core Java and ecosystem discussions.
B.4 Salle Petit Paris — Mixed topics room covering a broad range of subjects.
B.5 Versailles — Software Engineering track; focused on practices, testing, and craft.
B.6 CircusCelebration — AI & Testing talks; the go-to room for artificial intelligence and quality topics.
B.7 Das Studio — DevOps & Tooling sessions; build systems, CI/CD, and developer productivity.
B.8 Traumpalast — Hands-on workshops and extended coding sessions.
B.9 Jeanne d'Arc — Extended sessions and deep-dive talks that run longer than the standard slot.
B.10 Magic Cinema — Security & Advanced topics; cryptography, application security, and advanced JVM features.
B.11 Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie) — Community coding space where attendees contribute to open-source projects together.
B.12 Goethe — Home of the legendary Mario Kart tournament.
B.13 Heinrich Mack — Board games and Magic: The Gathering evenings.

§C Must-See Talks — Tuesday March 10
C.1 08:30 "Secure AI agents with Quarkus LangChain4j" by Willem Jan Glerum — CircusCelebration — Learn how to build secure AI agents using Quarkus and LangChain4j with guardrails and RAG.
C.2 08:30 "The road to JUnit 6" by Marc Philipp — Das Studio — The JUnit maintainer shares what is coming in the next major version of the testing framework.
C.3 10:00 KEYNOTE "AI: It AI-n't What You Think!" by Dr. Venkat Subramaniam — Dome — A keynote exploring common misconceptions about AI and what it really means for developers.
C.4 11:00 "Scaling Integration Tests" by Marvin Rensing — Versailles — Strategies and patterns for running integration tests at scale in large codebases.
C.5 12:00 "AI Testing Beyond the Basics" by Mario-Leander Reimer — Dome — Going beyond simple unit tests when building AI-powered applications.
C.6 12:00 "The Trust Trap - Security von Coding Assistants" by Clemens Hübner — Magic Cinema — Security implications and risks of using AI coding assistants in your workflow.
C.7 13:00 "Modern Java - Ask Me Anything" by Nicolai Parlog — Sanssouci — An interactive AMA session about modern Java features and where the language is heading.
C.8 13:00 "The Past, the Present, and the Future of Enterprise Java" by Ivar Grimstad — Dome — A journey through Enterprise Java from J2EE to Jakarta EE and beyond.
C.9 14:00 "Exploring Structured Concurrency and Scoped Values in Java 25" by Hanno Embregts — Magic Cinema — Deep dive into Project Loom's structured concurrency and scoped values arriving in Java 25.
C.10 16:00 "Spring AI trifft MCP" by Bernhard Löwenstein, Ionida Löwenstein — Bellevue — How Spring AI integrates with the Model Context Protocol for building AI applications.
C.11 17:00 "AI-Powered Form Wizards: Chat, Click, Done" by Loïc Magnette — Das Studio — Building intelligent form experiences powered by conversational AI.

§D Must-See Talks — Wednesday March 11
D.1 09:00 "Observing Project Valhalla" by Prof. Dr. Cay Horstmann — Dome — An in-depth look at value types and Project Valhalla's progress toward the JVM.
D.2 09:00 "AI-driven reverse engineering" by Martin Toshev — Das Studio — Using AI techniques to reverse-engineer and understand legacy systems.
D.3 10:00 "Vector Search Made Simple" by Dotan Horovits — Das Studio — Practical introduction to vector search and embeddings for Java developers.
D.4 11:00 Community Keynote: Live-Awards, Voting & Community — Dome — The beloved community keynote with live awards, audience voting, and celebration of the Java community.
D.5 12:00 "Bootiful Spring Boot: A DOGumentary" by Josh Long — Dome — Josh Long's entertaining and informative take on the latest in Spring Boot.
D.6 12:30 "Agentic Coding mit Java" by Frederik Wystup — Traumpalast — Building agentic coding workflows and AI-assisted development tools with Java.
D.7 13:00 "Local LLMs to Enterprise AI" by Marta Blaszczyk, Marco Klaassen — Dome — The journey from running local language models to deploying enterprise-grade AI solutions.
D.8 14:00 "The Evolution of Design Patterns in the Age of AI" by Miroslav Wengner — Dome — How classic design patterns are evolving as AI reshapes software architecture.
D.9 16:00 "From CRUD to Cognitive" by Ed Burns — Dome — Transitioning traditional CRUD applications into cognitive, AI-enhanced systems.

§E Evening Activities
E.1 Mario Kart Tournament — Tuesday 11:00-21:30 in the Goethe room. Sign up with Stefan Nachtrab! A JavaLand tradition with fierce competition.
E.2 Magic: The Gathering — Tuesday and Wednesday evenings in Heinrich Mack. Bring your decks or borrow from the community collection.
E.3 Board Games Night — Tuesday and Wednesday evenings in Heinrich Mack. A wide selection of board games for a relaxed evening.
E.4 Java macht Musik! — Tuesday 17:30-19:00 in Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie). Live music performed by members of the Java community.

§F Phantasialand Rides (for between sessions!)
F.1 Taron — Multi-launch coaster through the stunning Klugheim area. Widely regarded as one of the best coasters in Europe.
F.2 FLY — The world's longest flying coaster. A smooth and immersive ride experience through Rookburgh.
F.3 Chiapas — Log flume ride featuring one of the steepest drops in the world. You will get wet!
F.4 Black Mamba — An intense inverted coaster that flies through temple ruins. Smooth but thrilling.
F.5 Mystery Castle — A drop tower ride full of surprises inside a dark castle. Not for the faint-hearted.

§G Food & Drinks at Phantasialand
G.1 Rutmors — Themed restaurant located in the Klugheim area near the Taron coaster. Great atmosphere for a sit-down meal.
G.2 Quick Bites — Various food stalls are scattered throughout the park offering snacks and fast food.
G.3 Conference Catering — Lunch is provided between sessions near the Dome. Included with your conference ticket.
G.4 Coffee Stations — Available in all session building lobbies. Essential fuel between talks.

§H Practical Tips
H.1 WiFi is available in all session rooms. Connect to the JavaLand network for internet access during sessions.
H.2 The Dev Lounge at the Hackergarten (Foyer Galerie) is a great spot for networking and coding between talks.
H.3 Don't miss the Community Keynote on Wednesday at 11:00 in the Dome — it features live awards and audience voting.
H.4 Thursday workshops require separate registration. Check the JavaLand website for availability.
H.5 Brühl is easily accessible from Cologne Hauptbahnhof, about 20 minutes by regional train.
H.6 Wear comfortable shoes — you will be walking between theme park areas to reach different session rooms.
H.7 The JavaLand app has the full schedule with room maps and personal agenda features.
